Where AI Goes Wrong

The algorithms shaping your life. Are they fair?

From job applications to loan approvals to bail decisions — AI is already in the room. FairTrust exists to prove it's playing fair when it gets there.

The Problem

AI is making life-changing decisions. Is yours fair?

Hiring, lending, healthcare, criminal justice — hidden bias in AI systems is causing real harm, today. There's no universal standard to prove otherwise.

Amazon

Scrapped a hiring AI that penalised women

Resumes containing the word "women's" (e.g. women's chess club) were systematically downranked — learned entirely from historical data.

Banks

Charging minorities higher rates

Loan approval AIs charge higher interest rates or reject minority applicants — even with identical financial profiles.

Hidden

Bias buried deep in model mathematics

The bias is never programmed in — it's learned from historical data. Removing a sensitive column doesn't remove the information.

India

No AI Act. No enforcement. No standard.

Unlike the EU, India has no law requiring companies to prove their AI is fair. FairTrust fills both technical and policy gaps.

Real-World Impact

Where biased AI strikes hardest

These aren't hypothetical edge cases — they're live systems making decisions about real people, right now.

Hiring

Minority candidates screened out before humans see them

Resume-screening AIs learn from historical hires — amplifying the same biases they were meant to replace.

Prediction

Risk scores doubly penalise underrepresented groups

Predictive models in healthcare and insurance systematically underestimate risk for some and overestimate for others.

Banking

Higher loan rates charged to minority applicants

Even with identical credit profiles, loan AIs assign higher interest rates using proxy attributes like zip code.

Classification

34% higher error rates on darker-skinned faces

Image classifiers trained on non-representative data fail disproportionately across demographic groups.

Litigation

Courts and regulators are starting to act

The EU AI Act mandates fairness audits for high-risk systems. Companies without compliance documentation face legal exposure.

Proposed Solution

The JEDI Ethical AI Evaluation Framework

FairTrust is a comprehensive, end-to-end framework that evaluates, explains, and certifies AI models across seven fairness dimensions — producing compliance reports that regulators, developers, and end users can all understand.

Seven-Dimension Bias Detection

Individual, group, demographic, calibration, disparate impact, counterfactual, and intersectional bias — all scored independently.

SHAP & LIME Explainability

Make invisible decisions visible with individual and group-level feature attribution, exposing exactly where and why bias exists.

Prescriptive Remediation

Not just detection — actionable fixes including rebalancing, proxy removal, recalibration, and fairness-aware thresholds.

Machine-Readable JEDI Certificate

A compliance artifact your regulators and clients can verify — built for India's emerging AI governance landscape.
